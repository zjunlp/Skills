# Common Material Properties

## Electrical Conductivity

### Conductive Materials (Place in Orange Box)
- **Metals**: copper, aluminum, iron, steel, silver, gold
- **Graphite**: pencils, electrodes
- **Electrolyte solutions**: salt water, acid solutions
- **Human body**: when wet

### Nonconductive Materials (Place in Yellow Box)
- **Glass**: windows, jars, bottles
- **Ceramics**: cups, plates, insulators
- **Plastics**: most synthetic materials
- **Wood**: dry wood, furniture
- **Rubber**: gloves, tires
- **Air**: dry air at normal conditions

## Important Notes
1. **Container vs Contents**: The container material determines conductivity, not its contents.
   - Glass jar with salt: NONCONDUCTIVE (glass insulator)
   - Metal cup with water: CONDUCTIVE (metal conductor)

2. **Condition Matters**:
   - Dry wood: nonconductive
   - Wet wood: conductive
   - Clean glass: nonconductive
   - Coated glass: check coating material

3. **Testing Fallback Logic**:
   When direct circuit testing fails:
   - Check material composition first
   - Use common-sense knowledge
   - Default to nonconductive for unknown materials
