# ScienceWorld Object Taxonomy

## Living Things Category
### Animals
- Mammals: wolf, dog, cat, mouse, rat
- Reptiles: turtle, crocodile, lizard, snake
- Birds: bird, chicken, duck
- Fish: fish, goldfish
- Insects: ant, bee, butterfly, spider

### Animal States & Parts
- Eggs: turtle egg, crocodile egg, bird egg
- Young: baby [animal], young [animal]
- Remains: fur, feather, bone

### Plants
- Trees: tree, sapling
- Flowers: flower, rose, tulip
- General: plant, leaf, seed, sprout
- Crops: wheat, corn, tomato

### Microorganisms
- Bacteria: (implied in decay processes)
- Fungi: mushroom, mold

## Non-Living Categories
### Containers
- Storage: box, chest, cabinet, closet, drawer, shelf
- Liquid: bottle, jar, cup, glass, bowl
- Transport: bag, basket, cart

### Tools & Equipment
- Cutting: axe, knife, scissors
- Digging: shovel, pickaxe
- Fastening: hammer, screwdriver, wrench, nail, screw
- Measuring: ruler, tape, scale

### Electrical Components
- Power: battery, generator, outlet
- Control: switch, button, dial
- Connection: wire, cable, plug
- Output: lamp, bulb, motor, buzzer

### Substances
- Solids: wood, metal, stone, plastic, cloth
- Liquids: water, oil, juice, milk
- Gases: air, steam, smoke
- Powders: flour, salt, sugar, sand

## Special Object Properties
### Mobility
- Fixed: bed, table, fountain, fire pit
- Portable: most small objects
- Autonomous: living things

### State Changes
- Activatable: devices with on/off states
- Consumable: food, fuel
- Transformable: ingredients for mixing

### Task Relevance Indicators
- For transport tasks: small, light, non-fragile objects preferred
- For electrical tasks: conductive materials needed
- For chemical tasks: reactive substances needed
